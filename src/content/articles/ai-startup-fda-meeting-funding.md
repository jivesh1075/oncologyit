## Why This FDA Meeting Is Different

Most startups meet the FDA when they have something to submit — a 510(k), De Novo, or PMA application. This meeting is happening **before product development is complete**. That's unprecedented.

What they're discussing: **regulatory pathway optimization**. Not "here's our product, please approve it." But "here's what we're building — how do we design it to sail through your review process?"

This is regulatory arbitrage at scale. By getting FDA input during development, they can:
1. Design studies that meet regulatory requirements from day one
2. Avoid the 12-18 month delays that kill most AI startups
3. Build their evidence package concurrently with product development
4. Secure breakthrough device designation before competitors even start trials

The $50M funding round isn't for R&D. It's for **regulatory acceleration**. They're buying time — the one resource no startup can create.

Conventional wisdom says AI oncology companies should:
1. Build algorithm
